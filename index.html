Kelvingrove Art Gallery

Kelvingrove Museum opened in 1901 and is a firm favourite with local people and visitors. It has stunning architecture and a family friendly atmosphere. Explore our 22 galleries and discover everything from art to animals, Ancient Egypt to Charles Rennie Mackintosh and so much more. We also have a changing programme of temporary exhibitions and displays. FREE ENTRY

Hunterian Museum

The Hunterian is Scotland’s oldest public museum and home to over a million magnificent items ranging from meteorites to mummies and Mackintosh. Within this diverse collection you will find astounding artefacts, amazing art and an astonishing array of animal life. ADMITION to anatomy is bookings only. admission fee is £6.00 adult £3.00 child. Museums holds rocks/minerals, dinsosaurs/fossils, zoology, scientific instruments, anatomy/medicine, culture/archeology and fine arts.

Glasgow Botanic Gardens

Glasgow Botanic Gardens, located in the West End of Glasgow and managed by Glasgow City Council is arguably the finest Garden in Glasgow, and is dominated by the recently restored Kibble Palace. The Kibble Palace. a magnificent Curvilinular Glasshouse designed by John Kibble (full details elsewhere in this site) houses the National collection of tree ferns. Murals Trip

Discover a different piece of art. The Glasgow murals showcase a diverse range of talent and styles and are now recognised as unique pieces of art in their own right. Murals are painted by local artist from Glasgow. Among the 13+ murals you will see include: ‘Fellow Glasgow Residents’ - Smug ‘The World’s Most Economical Taxi’ - RogueOne ‘Glasgow Panda’ - Klingatron/James Kling, This tour runs every Friday, Saturday and Sunday at 2pm. https://www.glasgow.gov.uk/CHttpHandler.ashx?id=19649 https://www.walkingtoursin.com/street-art-tour
